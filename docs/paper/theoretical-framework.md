# Theoretical Framework

HariKnows can be explained using the Technology Acceptance Model (TAM) and the Information Systems Success Model.

## Technology Acceptance Model
TAM is relevant because the platform depends on whether students and staff perceive the system as useful and easy to use.

- Perceived usefulness: students get faster answers and request visibility.
- Perceived ease of use: a simple chatbot and account page reduce friction.
- Behavioral intention: more reliable support encourages adoption.

## Information Systems Success Model
This model helps explain why the system should be accurate, usable, and trusted.

- System quality: stable login, request tracking, and CSV import behavior.
- Information quality: correct registrar context, clear policies, and validated account data.
- Service quality: dependable helpdesk guidance and account management.
- Net benefits: less manual work, faster information access, and improved service delivery.

## Application to HariKnows
The system is designed to be useful only when its answers are grounded in verified registrar data, its flows are easy to follow, and its outputs remain consistent with privacy rules and office policy.
